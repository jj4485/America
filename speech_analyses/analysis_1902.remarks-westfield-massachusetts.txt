This speech references America by affirming its grounding principle of citizen education and the key role it plays in the functioning of the Republic. In addition, it points to the American public school system and lauds its structure that promotes higher education for all who desire it. By highlighting the importance of character formation alongside formal education, the speaker underscores the pluralistic aspects of an American citizen's formation. Furthermore, the mention of solving problems "presented to us" indicates a collective, national endeavor, signaling America. Lastly, by placing emphasis on the critical roles of various societal units (the home, the school, etc.) in molding a citizen, the speaker refers to an integrated approach, a broad reflection of American society's ethos.
