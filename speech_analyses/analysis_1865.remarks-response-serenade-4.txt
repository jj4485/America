This speech references America as a country undergoing a significant change, specifically with regards to the abolishment of slavery. The speaker identifies America as a nation that can congratulate itself and the whole world on its moral victory of passing the Constitutional amendment for this purpose. Moreover, the speech refers to America as made up of individual States, who have the responsibility to vote and thereby consummate what Congress has started. The overall tone suggests a sense of national pride in taking these steps towards ending slavery, along with an acknowledge of a united America whose future harmony relies on the eradication of the 'original disturbing cause' (slavery). By mentioning specific states like Illinois and Maryland, the speech offers a more detailed picture of the nation's progress in this endeavor.
