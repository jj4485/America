This speech references America by highlighting its ideals through the example of Vermont. The speaker presents Vermont as a representative of the American ideal, characterized by the quality of its citizens rather than its material resources. The speaker also touches on the ethos of hard work and resilience in facing difficulties, which he suggests is a crucial element of American citizenship. The speech cites the qualities of self-reliance, courage in times of war, and the virtue of preserving over seeking mere ease as values that are inherent to American identity. Thus, in emphasizing these characteristics, the speech presents the vision of America as a nation of strong, tenacious, responsible, and dedicated individuals.
