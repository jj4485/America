This speech references America, referred to as the United States, as a nation that values peace, prosperity, happiness, and aggrandizement without causing conflict with the welfare, safety, and rights of other nations. It represents America as a nation with no enemies, animosities, or rivalries. It speaks of the United States as a friend to all nations but with a special friendship for nations that, like the United States itself, are founded on the principles of equal rights. The speech further underscores America's cooperation with neighboring nations to advance civilization and culture on the American continent. Ultimately, the speech seeks to communicate America's commitment to dealing justly, frankly, and liberally with others, specifically mentioning Peru in this context.
