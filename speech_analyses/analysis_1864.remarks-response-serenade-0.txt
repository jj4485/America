This speech references America through its discussion of the country's Civil War. Mentioned are significant figures from the Union side of the conflict, such as Sheridan and General Grant, indicating that the speaker supports the Union in this split America. The speaker further suggests a strong nationalistic sentiment by calling for cheers for the soldiers, sailors, and supporters of the Union. Lastly, the term "Secesh" used as a nickname for the Confederates, emphasizes the internal division within America at that time.
