This speech references America in several ways. Firstly, it refers to the United States as a republic and to its system of government, highlighting the strength required of a government to maintain its existence, particularly in times of great emergencies. It emphasizes the importance of elections to demonstrate the functioning of free government, bolstering its assertion with its experience of a presidential election in the midst of a rebellion. 

It also makes a considerable reference to the country's conditions during the civil war and utilizes this context to underline the resilience and unity of the American people. It mentions "our common country," indicating a shared national identity and interest among all citizens. 

Moreover, the speech uses patriotic language, mentioning the role of brave, patriotic men in the country's strength and stability. Lastly, it ends by expressing appreciation for American soldiers and seamen, as well as their commanders, further augmenting the speech's overall references to America.
