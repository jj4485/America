This speech references America as a vast, rugged wilderness successfully tamed and cultivated by its pioneering citizens. It alludes to the American expansion across the continent and gives a nod to the American West, specifically mentioning the areas of sthe Black Hills, Deadwood, and Lead, significant sites during the 19th century gold rush. The speech also acknowledges the American heritage and spirit of perseverance, ingenuity, and strength, emphasizing the role of strong, robust pioneers in shaping America's progress. Finally, the "conquering of a continent" not only for themselves but also for their race and people can be seen as a reference to America's growth and expansion as a nation.
