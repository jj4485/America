This speech references America by addressing significant events and issues occurring in the United States at the time, specifically Abraham Lincoln's second Presidential Inaugural in 1865. The speech deals with the Civil War which is significantly absorbing the country's energies and attention, speaking to the ongoing division and strife within the nation. It also highlights the issue of slavery, particularly focused in the South, as a central cause of the war and a critical concern for America's present and future. The address references America's values and ideals, calling for unity, charity, and a commitment to peace. It also alludes to the nation's religious faith, invoking God as a guide and judge in the midst of conflict. Implying that American slavery is an offense that God is willing to remove symbolizes a turning point in American history and society. Finally, it encourages to heal the nation's wounds and establish lasting peace, thus painting a hopeful picture for America's future.
