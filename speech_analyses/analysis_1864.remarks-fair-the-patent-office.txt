This speech references America in several ways. Firstly, it speaks of an 'extraordinary war' occurring within its borders which deeply affects all its citizens, specifically highlighting the immense sacrifices of American soldiers. Moreover, it acknowledges the unique developments within this war such as the organization of relief fairs for suffering soldiers and their families. The speech also specifically recognizes the women of America as chief agents of these fairs and praises their conduct during this war, stating that even the highest praises of poets and orators could not do them justice. The speaker ends by offering a blessing to American women. In all these ways, this speech directly references the contributions and spirit of the American people during a time of conflict.
