Sentence 1: 1931 The Presidents News Conference 914

PRIME MINISTER RICHARD B. BENNETT
THE PRESIDENT.
Sentence 2: I have been very glad to welcome here today the Canadian Premier on his informal visit to Washington.
Sentence 3: We have no formal matters under discussion.
Sentence 4: We are mutually interested in the common welfare of both nations, and we will have, no doubt, some informal conversations on problems of the future, and that sort of conversations always leads to better working understandings.
Sentence 5: I consider it a very great compliment that Mr. Bennett has found it possible to come to Washington in such a fashion.
Sentence 6: VIRGIN ISLANDS
Sentence 7: The only other matter of interest that I have today is that we have undertaken to reorganize the Government of the Virgin Islands.
Sentence 8: The Navy Department has asked to be relieved of the administration, and I am assigning it to the Department of the Interior.
Sentence 9: I am proposing to forward to Congress the name of Dr. Paul M. Pearson, of Pennsylvania, for Governor.
Sentence 10: Other civilian officials will be appointed in replacement of naval officers.
Sentence 11: Congress recently made some appropriations for reorganization and developmental work in the islands, and the new organization is intended to make that more effective.
Sentence 12: The Director of the Budget and the Director of the Bureau of Efficiency have worked out a plan for the new administration which will be carried into action by the Department of the Interior.
Sentence 13: QUESTIONS
Q. Mr. President, is that effective today--immediately?
THE PRESIDENT.
Sentence 14: Yes, I will send the name of the new Governor up to Congress, and as soon as he is confirmed or otherwise we will make the changes.
Sentence 15: Q. Mr. President, you spoke of Mr. Bennett having set a fashion in coming down here.
Sentence 16: THE PRESIDENT.
Sentence 17: I didn't say "set a fashion."
Sentence 18: I said "in such a fashion."
