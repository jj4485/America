Sentence 1: 1903 Remarks Surf California

My Fellow-Citizens:
I cannot say how much I have enjoyed these three days in California.
Sentence 2: It is the first time I ever was in your great and beautiful State; and but a few hours ago I saw for the first time the greatest of all the oceans.
Sentence 3: I have enjoyed it to the full.
Sentence 4: I have enjoyed the climate, seeing the fruits of the soil, seeing all that has been done agriculturally and industrially.
Sentence 5: I have enjoyed noting the marvelous material progress and prosperity; but what I have enjoyed most has been seeing the men and women of California.
Sentence 6: It has been to me an education to come here to California.
Sentence 7: I did not need to feel what I felt already, how much of our destiny lay on the Pacific, but I am glad to have seen your people.
Sentence 8: I have realized more even than I already realized it the fundamental oneness of the American, nation.
Sentence 9: I have come from the Atlantic across the continent, and here I am addressing an American audience with the same ideals, the same aspirations, the same hopes, the same purposes, that the audiences have on the Atlantic seaboard, or in the Mississippi Valley.
Sentence 10: I am glad to have met you.
Sentence 11: I believe in you with all my heart and soul, and I believe that your future will be even greater than your past.
