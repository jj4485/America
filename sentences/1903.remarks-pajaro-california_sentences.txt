Sentence 1: 1903 Remarks Pajaro California

My Fellow-Citizens:
I want to thank you for coming out to greet me this morning.
Sentence 2: I have been giving much more time to California than to any other State, and I am glad of it, for I have enjoyed every hour I have been in your beautiful and wonderful State.
Sentence 3: I have been traveling up from the South and shall now visit San Francisco, then go straight through to the North.
Sentence 4: It seems to me every good American that can should visit the Pacific Slope, to realize where so much of our country's greatness in the future will lie.
Sentence 5: I did not need to come out here in order to believe in you and your work.
Sentence 6: I knew you well and believed in you before with all my heart, but it has done me good to get in touch with you.
Sentence 7: The thing that has impressed me most coming from the Atlantic across to the Pacific has been that good Americans are good Americans in every part of this country.
Sentence 8: That is the fundamental point to remember.
Sentence 9: I am glad to have seen you.
Sentence 10: I want to welcome the men and women, and especially the children.
Sentence 11: Of course, it is a mere truism to say that this country depends upon what the next generation is.
