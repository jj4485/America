Sentence 1: 1905 Remarks Parsons Kansas

Friends:
I will just bid you good-morning.
Sentence 2: I am so glad to be again in the Sunflower State.
Sentence 3: From the beginning I have believed in your people, and I am more than grateful for the way your people showed your belief in me last November.
Sentence 4: It is not possible for any man in any position to promise that things will seem right.
Sentence 5: All that he can promise is that so far as in him lies he will do his part toward making them right.
Sentence 6: This I can promise, and do.
Sentence 7: It is in one stage of life as it is in another.
Sentence 8: Right here I see one of the men who fought in the great war.
Sentence 9: All that he could do in that war was to do his part well, to do all that lay in him well.
Sentence 10: And that is what each one of us can do, in whatever position he is called to be.
Sentence 11: It is because of what you and the average man and woman of Kansas have done that Kansas is so great a State; and it is that every man, from the President right through, has got to do if our country is to be what it should be.
Sentence 12: To you people in Kansas, to you in whom I believe so much, there are just one or two things I could say in this brief moment.
Sentence 13: There is nothing peculiar in the qualities you need in government that differentiates those qualities from the qualities you need in having one private man deal with another.
Sentence 14: If a man is to be an advantage to the community in which he lives he must be a decent, square dealing man, who does fairly by his neighbor and takes care of his family.
Sentence 15: The same qualities in another sphere must be applied in public life if he is to amount to anything-honesty, decency, courage, and common sense.
