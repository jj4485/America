Sentence 1: 1905 Remarks Clayton New Mexico
Sentence 2: It is a great pleasure to me to be back in New Mexico and to pass through Clayton.
Sentence 3: Right from this neighborhood, some fifteen of your citizens came into my regiment.
Sentence 4: Several of them were wounded.
Sentence 5: I have the feeling for them that one naturally has for the men with whom he has been thrown into the intimate relations of life.
Sentence 6: I am particularly pleased to see you all here.
Sentence 7: I am glad to see a first-class exhibit of New Mexico's children.
Sentence 8: You do not know what a pleasure it is to me to get out into this Western country, that I have known so well, where I have ranched in the other days, and from which I drew the men with whom I went to Santiago when the war with Spain broke out.
Sentence 9: I follow your career with the greatest possible interest.
Sentence 10: When I get out here and see the big plains, it makes me feel as if I were at home again.
